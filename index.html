The Problem

Technical ability isn't the issue.
Game intelligence is.

Most extra coaching gives players more repetitions of the same things lines, cones, isolated skills. It looks productive. But on match day, nothing changes. The pressure is different. The space is real. The decisions are theirs to make.

The players who struggle on match day aren't lacking technique. They're lacking the ability to read a game, back their instincts, and make decisions under pressure. That's not something you develop standing in a line. It's something you develop in environments that replicate what a match actually feels like.

That's what every session I run is built around.

About

Over 20 years developing players the right way.

I'm Kurtis, a football development coach based in Birmingham. For over two decades I've worked with players at every level from grassroots to JPL, academy environments and international youth football.

My approach is built on one principle: players develop when they're put in environments that challenge them to think, not just move. Every session I run is designed around real game situations, realistic pressure, and decision-making. No drills. No standing in lines. No shouting left and right.

The players on my programmes don't just improve in training. They show up differently on match day. That's the only result that matters.
